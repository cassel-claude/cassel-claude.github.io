How the freelance rate is calculated

Revenue needed = (target income + expenses) × (1 + margin). Your hourly rate = revenue needed ÷ (billable hours per week × weeks worked per year). The margin matters: as a freelancer you pay self-employment tax and absorb slow months, so a buffer of 20–30% is normal.

Why is my rate higher than a salary equivalent?

An employee's salary is only part of their cost — employers also pay benefits, taxes, equipment, and downtime. As a freelancer you cover all of that yourself, so your hourly rate has to be meaningfully higher than a salaried hourly equivalent.
