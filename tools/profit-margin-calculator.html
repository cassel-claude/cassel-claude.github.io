Margin vs. markup — what's the difference?

Profit margin is profit as a percentage of the price: (price − cost) ÷ price. Markup is profit as a percentage of the cost: (price − cost) ÷ cost. A 50% margin is an 100% markup — they describe the same dollar of profit from two different angles, which is why they're so easy to confuse.

Which should I use to set prices?

Use margin when you're thinking about how much of each sale you keep, and markup when you're pricing up from a known cost. The "target margin → price" tab does the markup math for you.
